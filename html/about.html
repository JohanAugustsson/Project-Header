Vanishing Rodent was founded in 1984 by Ruben ”the Floppy” Simonsson and Greger ”Monocrome” Hansson.

In those times, sources for programmers were very limited. ”You had to start from scratch with about everything”, recalls ”the Floppy”.

Programming was done in C and Assembler with really heavy compilations. Hardware equipment originally consisted of two Amiga 500, one Epson matrix printer and four pocketheld calculators (!).

Vanishing Rodent was originally inhabitated in ”Monocrome's” garage, which was a real challenge during wintertime due to the mice who chew on the cables (this could sometimes prove fatal to the mice!) to the computers.
After the success with the first game “Rat tormentor” (1985) Vanishing Rodent could afford moving into more suitable locals and also upgrade their equipment.

Through the blockbusters “Micecracker” (1987) and “Cat invasion” (1988) Vanishing Rodent definitely established themselves in the hall of fame of gameproducers.

Today (November 2017) Vanishing Rodent consists of 25 employees. Through its consistent sensitivity towards the market it has been able not only to keep, but also increase it's market share in the genre retro and indie-games.

Vision: Through close contact with the end customer and with sustained/growing creativity being able to continually grow it’s market share within our genre.
